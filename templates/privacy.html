Clause 2: Nature of the Platform and Research Context

This platform is a research prototype for a university Mission Capstone project. It is not a clinical, diagnostic, or certified educational tool. Procrastination risk scores are statistical indicators for supportive MCII-based (Mental Contrasting with Implementation Intentions) intervention resources and aggregated, anonymised risk trend information for course facilitators.

Ethical significance: Prevents misuse of outputs; establishes that predictions are not definitive diagnoses and cannot be used for academic penalties, consistent with UNESCO AI Ethics Recommendations.

Clause 3: Data Collected

The platform collects:

  • (a) Behavioural engagement data: Login timestamps, session duration, assignment interaction frequency.
  • (b) Chatbot conversation logs: Interactions with the MCII AI chatbot.
  • (c) Account information: A unique participant code assigned at registration (not real name, student ID, or email).

Personal identifiers are separated from behavioural data at collection and replaced with participant codes. It does not collect financial, health, or sensitive personal data.

Ethical significance: Adheres to data minimisation required by Rwanda Law No. 058/2021, GDPR, and NDPA 2023.

Clause 4: How Your Data is Used

Behavioural data is used exclusively to:

  • (a) Generate daily personalised procrastination risk predictions.
  • (b) Provide tailored MCII intervention prompts.
  • (c) Contribute to aggregated, anonymised research summaries and cohort-level trend reporting for course facilitators, where applicable.

Ethical significance: Limits processing to stated purposes and supports transparency for participants and facilitators in line with lawful basis and purpose limitation principles under applicable data protection law.
